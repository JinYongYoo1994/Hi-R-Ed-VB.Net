Wounds have long required significant time and resources in order to heal and to prevent additional complications from occurring. It is the responsibility of the clinician to provide proper care, including education of the patient and caregiver(s). Clinicians need to have the ability to identify the type of wound, causative factors, and appropriate interventions. The strides made in wound care products and therapies have taken a giant step forward in the past several years. There are many treatments, both simple and complex, available. Clinicians providing care to patients with wounds must continue to learn new skills and techniques, in order to treat the patient in the most effective manner. Adequate nutrition, compliance to ordered medical treatments, the ability to recognize risk factors, and other preventative measures are the best methods.
